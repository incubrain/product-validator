## My Story

Design theatre stole years—WordPress logo loops, "add one more page" to ghost
products, CSS pixel-nudging instead of customer validation. PT studio
split-shift left zero marinating hours, just execution treadmill. Sales job:
1-2hr drives each way to door-slams, time on road thinking but not building.
Police: 6 days/week, unpredictable rotations, paperwork juggling with ADHD
brain—nightmare with no routine.

Hacks worked temporarily: batch cooking bought back 4hr/week, minimalist
room-existence eliminated social time-drains, laundry truck job (20hr/week,
brain-off, podcast-fueled learning). But one knock equals collapse.

Geographic arbitrage was the unlock: Thailand (2018) → Philippines (2019) →
India (2022). Each drop in cost of living lowered the "enough" bar. India now:
**~$3.3k USD median US income equals ~₹3 lakh/month here**. My comfort (₹1
lakh) + 2 employees (₹1 lakh) funded by validator $3k USD MRR target. Time isn't
bought with money alone—it's bought by deleting (design theatre vetoed),
delegating (household help ₹25k/mo in India), and lowering enough (India
arbitrage equals more brain-hours affordable).

Time is freedom's proxy; hoard it ruthlessly by deleting mental clutter first,
or your brain-business starves.

## Mental Time (Free Fuel)

This costs nothing but awareness. Your skull's rented to ghosts—open loops,
clutter-fog, tiny choices bleeding focus. Reclaim it.

**Delete first. Always delete first.**

Can you delete this task? Yes? Gone. No? Can you delegate? Yes? Outsource. No?
Then optimize—never sooner. Delete > Delegate > Optimize. That's the hierarchy.
Best part is no part.

Open loops live rent-free in your skull—reply to that email, fix that drawer,
decide on that thing. Close 3 today, buy back 10 minutes of clarity tomorrow. My
loops? Notification tigers, open-tab zoos, meeting fog—auditing them freed 15
hours/week for the forge. Your leaks? Different scars, same cage.

Remove clutter from spaces you inhabit—home, car, office. Create systems so it
doesn't return. Remove small decisions, especially mornings. Limit options: 3
choices beats 10 for speed. Block time. Restrict caffeine. Limit alcohol.
Improve sleep, diet, exercise—sharp vault-hours beat foggy busywork marathons.

## Physical Time (Bought Back)

This costs money but scales you. Outsource the predictable, the trivial, the
soul-draining.

Buy back your time: Cleaning, cooking, admin—delegate. Cut work hours if
possible. Reduce expenses that chain you to grind. Limit screen time that
doesn't fuel the forge. Block time for relationships that charge you, veto the
ones that drain.

Every yes to them is a no to you. Practice the veto: "No, that meeting's a
leak." "No, that favor's a cage." Reclaim sovereignty, one refusal at a time.

Reclaimed hours? Prototype your scar-solution. Test the validator's first
traffic strategy. Without time, the forge is theory—with it, it's shipping. The
product validator isn't just philosophy—it needs hours to execute, to iterate,
to validate against your daily use.

Buying time without knowing what you're buying it FOR? That's the whim-trap.
Next: etch the compass these hours serve.

## Takeaway

13 years leaked to time thieves: design loops, split-shifts, unpredictable
police chaos, isolation building without feedback. Geographic arbitrage
(India 2022) finally clicked—lower cost equals lower enough equals more
brain-hours. Batch cooking, minimalist cuts, household delegation—all bought
time. But medication (stimulant switch 2024) bought consistency: hacks stopped
collapsing. Audit your thieves, veto the theatre, move if needed.
